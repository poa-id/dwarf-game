# Tinkering & Gemcutting

Tinkering is the newest of the five active skills. It currently has exactly one action — cutting gems — with jewelry and gadgets deferred to later content.

## Gems

Rough gems are a **Mining** byproduct — see [Mining](Mining.md) for drop chances per vein. Cutting has a real success/failure chance; a failed cut wastes the rough gem entirely, no XP, no cut gem.

| | Base success chance |
|---|---|
| Cutting any rough gem | 60% |

Cutting always grants 10 XP per *attempt*, but the XP only lands on success.

| Rough | Cut | Rarity tier |
|---|---|---|
| Rough Quartz | Cut Quartz | Common (from copper veins) |
| Rough Garnet | Cut Garnet | Uncommon (from iron veins) |
| Rough Amethyst | Cut Amethyst | Rare (from deepstone) |

## Building the Gemcutting station

| | Cost |
|---|---|
| Build | 800 Insight + 15 Copper Ingot + 20 Wood |

Sits in the Hearth Hall near the copper vein (not the Forge Room) — gems are a mining byproduct, so the station belongs near where you already gather.

## Gemcutting station tiers

Each tier raises **both** raw gem-drop chance (feeding back into Mining) and cutting-success chance together — one combined track.

| Tier | Name | Insight cost | Drop chance bonus | Cutting success bonus |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Steadier Hands | 300 | +0.5% | +10% |
| 2 | Loupe and Wheel | 700 | +1% | +20% |
| 3 | Master's Bench | 1,500 | +1.5% | +30% |

## Tinkering's perk tree

Spent with **cut gems** — a third permanent-multiplier currency, separate from Insight and True-metals. Boosts both gem-drop chance and cutting success further, on top of the station's own tier — the self-reinforcing loop: more gems found → more cut → bigger bonus → easier to find and cut the next batch.

Mirrors the shape of the Smelter's XP perk tree and the Hearth's yield perk tree (1/3/6 cumulative spend → +5%/+10%/+15%).
