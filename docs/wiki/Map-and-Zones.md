# Map & Zones

One fixed, hand-placed 80×50 map — not procedurally generated, no scrolling camera, no fast travel. The Hearth sits at the psychological and literal center. The mountain grows through *revelation* (uncovering hidden halls) rather than expanding outward.

## Zones

| Zone | Unlock condition | Contains |
|---|---|---|
| **The Hearth Hall** (central) | Always open | The Hearth, Mountain Console, copper vein, root tangle, Charcoal Kiln, Gemcutting station, Narag-Bund |
| **Forge Room** | Always walkable (forge itself starts broken) | The Forge, the Smelter |
| **The Mine** | Always open | Iron vein (N), Deepstone Seam (mid), Copper vein (SW), Coal Seam (SE), the Mine Shaft |
| **The Garden** | Always open (broken planters to repair) | 6 planter slots, wood root, kiln |
| **Tinkering Room** | Forge tier 2+ | Gemcutting station |
| **The Stockpile** *(sealed, east)* | Rubble-blocked until cleared | Shared ore stockpile chest |
| **Trade Hall** *(sealed, south)* | Rubble-blocked, requires restoration score 2,000 | Merchant post |
| **Deep Foundry** *(sealed, northwest)* | Rubble-blocked, requires restoration score 4,000 | Deepstone smelting content |
| **The Archive** *(sealed, north)* | Rubble-blocked, requires restoration score 6,000 | Mountain lore/history |

Cells outside any named zone are open connective hallway, always passable — only specific rooms gate behind progress, to avoid an early-game maze of invisible walls.

## Torches

Three fixed corridor torches exist from the start, repaired by walking adjacent and pressing E (cost: copper ingots), permanently lit once repaired. You can also place your own torches anywhere on a wall (T — costs 1 wood + 1 coal to place, then E to light with a copper ingot).

## Lighting

All unlocked zones are always fully lit — there's no fog-of-war or line-of-sight system currently active. Torches and fire structures add a warm color overlay but don't gate visibility.

## Movement philosophy

Walking is the way the mountain remains a *place* rather than a menu. As more of the map opens up, the intended fix for tedious travel is always an in-world object you walk to and use — mine carts, lifts, rail networks — never a "teleport to Forge" dropdown or fast-travel list.
